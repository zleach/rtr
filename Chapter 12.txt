Emily finds herself stranded at the end of a long day—her car failing her just when she craves the comforts of home. As her mechanic husband comes to her aid, a seemingly ordinary day takes a sharp turn when the airwaves of the auto shop carry the voice of State Representative David Rutledge—a man deeply entwined with Emily's quest for justice under the shadow of the 30th Amendment. With the announcement of a series of town hall meetings aimed at discussing the future of this controversial law, Emily realizes that her personal struggle has catapulted into the public domain, where the lines between truth and political maneuvering blur. As the chapter unfolds, the radio's conservative talk paints a chilling picture of the battle Emily will face—a battle that extends far beyond the courtroom and into the very values that Texans hold dear.
